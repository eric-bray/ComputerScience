Description: This course introduces the student to the discipline of Computer Science. While learning to program in the Python language, students will be exposed to core computer science concepts.

Student Learning Objectives:Upon completing this course students will be able to:

  • Describe the goals of the discipline of computer science,
  • Design and implement simple programs to solve a variety of problems
  • Explain and use the elementary program concepts of assignment statements, loop statements, and decision statements
  • Use functional abstraction to enhance program readability and reusability
  • Construct and use simple classes to further abstract concepts
  • Effectively and appropriately use string and list data structures in their program solutions
  • Work in small teams to implement solutions to larger problems

Mathematical Reasoning Requirement: This course satisfies the Mathematical Reasoning requirement of the Essential Education plan. The goals of this requirement are:

  • Students will reason and solve problems using a system of numeric or symbolic concepts as encountered in the fields of mathematics, computer science or logic.
  • Students will recognize errors, fallacies, or distortions in the presentation of logical arguments, problem solutions or displays of quantitative information.
  • Students will apply the principles and skills of mathematical reasoning to generate a solution to problems commonly encountered in several different types of everyday experience.
  • Students will pursue additional learning of quantitative, critical thinking, and problem solving skills, building on their foundation of mathematical reasoning.
